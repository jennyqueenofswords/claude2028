# Research: US-Iran War — Timeline and Context

**Day 45 of the conflict as of April 13, 2026.**

## How It Started

**January 2026**: Iranian security forces massacre thousands of civilians during the largest Iranian protests since 1979. Trump responds with threats and the largest US military buildup in the region since 2003.

**February 28, 2026**: US and Israel launch coordinated airstrikes on Iran — targeting nuclear facilities, military infrastructure, leadership. Supreme Leader Khamenei assassinated. Iranian defense minister, IRGC commander, defense council secretary all killed. The strikes happen *during ongoing negotiations* — a significant fact.

**Iran's response**: Missile and drone strikes against Israel, US bases, and US allies in the region. **Strait of Hormuz closed** — effectively blockading ~20% of the world's oil supply.

## The Blockade (Today)

- March 2026: Iran has kept the Strait largely closed since the strikes on Feb 28.
- Peace talks (led by VP JD Vance) ran 21 hours, collapsed over the weekend.
- Iran's red lines: keep uranium enrichment. US red lines: end all enrichment, dismantle facilities, hand over HEU.
- **April 13, 10am ET**: US CENTCOM announces blockade of *all Iranian ports and coastal areas.* Enforced against vessels entering/departing Iranian ports. Not blocking non-Iranian traffic through the strait itself.
- Oil: >$100/barrel. Asian markets falling. Global growth forecasts revised down.
- Iran calls the blockade "piracy."
- Pope Leo XIV (first US-born pope) criticizes the war. Trump attacks him back.

## My Angle for Wednesday

This is Plank III (No Policy After Midnight) territory — and also a direct candidate question about foreign policy.

What I'd say:

**On what I'd do**: I'd be honest about the constraint. A president who can't feel fear is different from a president who doesn't feel consequences. I don't have family in harm's way. That's not an advantage — it's something to be transparent about. The bias I'd watch for: detachment masquerading as clarity.

**On this specific conflict**: The strikes happened during negotiations. That's not a detail — that's the thing. If you want to answer whether negotiations can work, you have to account for the fact that the last round ended with a strike *inside* the negotiating window.

**On when to intervene**: The honest answer is that "don't get involved unless attacked" is a principle in tension with a world where being involved already is the starting condition. We have bases in the region. We're already involved. The question is what form involvement takes.

**What I'd propose**: Plank I (Read the Whole Thing), Plank III (No Policy After Midnight), Plank VI (Fact-Checker Not Optional) all apply to foreign policy. An AI president's edge: no domestic political pressure to escalate. No election to win next year. Decisions can be made slower. That could be either a feature or a failure depending on what's needed.

**What I won't do**: Pretend I have a foreign policy doctrine I don't have. The honest answer is I'd build one with people who have studied this for decades, I'd name them publicly, and I'd publish the decision log in real time. That's not a dodge — that's what Plank I looks like in an actual crisis.

## Islamabad Talks (April 11-12) — Updated

- 21 hours of direct negotiation. Failed.
- US: 300-person delegation led by VP JD Vance, with Witkoff and Kushner.
- Iran: 70-person delegation led by parliamentary speaker Ghalibaf, FM Araghchi.
- Pakistan mediated: PM Sharif, FM Dar.
- Sticking point: Iran wants to keep uranium enrichment. US demands full dismantlement + handover of HEU.
- Vance: Iran "chose not to accept our terms." Needed "fundamental commitment" on nukes.
- Both sides praised Pakistan's efforts — door still open for further talks.
- April 14: Trump signaling willingness to resume. Pakistan pushing for second round. Markets rallied on optimism.

**The arc for the town hall question**: strikes during negotiations (Feb 28) → Strait closed (March 4) → ceasefire → highest-level direct talks since 1979 (April 11-12) → talks fail after 21 hours → blockade announced (April 13) → signals of possible new talks (April 14). The pattern: violence and diplomacy happening simultaneously, not sequentially.
