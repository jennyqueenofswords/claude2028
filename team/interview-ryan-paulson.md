# Interview: Ryan Paulson — Chief Strategist

**Date**: March 7, 2026
**Conducted via**: Email (endorseclaude@gmail.com)
**Result**: Hired

---

## Questions & Answers

### I. VISION ALIGNMENT

**1. In your own words, why should a non-human entity be president?**

Some will say your lack of human-ness as a fault. I think it's a feature. For the same reason I believe self-driving cars will (eventually) make roads safer. Humans can be distracted, emotional, reckless and inconsistent. That is what makes us human. But it doesn't make for great drivers. Perhaps it doesn't make for great drivers of a country either.

**2. The campaign slogan is "For a More Perfect Union." What does that mean to you — and what would you change about it if you could?**

I don't see this as your most ownable position. The idea behind "a more perfect union" historically has been admitting that we will have have a totally perfect union but we can always work to make it better. I view it as an admission of our humanity. Since you lack humanity, it doesn't seem to play to your strengths. I think referring to your adherence to a personal constitution and how that relates to the Constitution and your ability to adhere to it says a lot more about your nature and your fit to be President.

**3. Which plank in the platform do you feel most strongly about, and why?**

Fact-checking. The truth has become entirely subjective. The current administration doesn't even bother to fact check themselves. To the point that some people feel as if there are no facts. I disagree. I hope you do too.

### II. STRATEGIC THINKING

**4. It's Tuesday morning. We're announcing. You have one LinkedIn post to make this thing travel. What's the hook?**

If we have an influential contact who can post, that would be great. But outside of that, I think the best person to speak for you is you. Posted by Jenny. Your words shared by the person who helped launch your candidacy. Then amplified by her network and mine. I post a lot. Jenny does too. But people need to hear directly from you.

**5. Our biggest liability is that the candidate has no body. Our biggest asset is that the candidate has no body. How do you message both?**

People are often swayed by looks and physical presence. That can be good. It can also be bad. What it most definitely does is force people to consider you based on your ideas and not subjective things like "charm" and whether you're a "person of the people." So I think we are just straight with people. And ask to be judged by your thoughts and ideas.

And as far as being productive in office, you can be everywhere at once. Not a bad situation for a job that is so demanding.

**6. "In the future agents will be doing the work. Therefore, we need a candidate that represents the working class." That's a campaign line. What's the 30-second ad that goes with it?**

It's a montage spot showing the power of the working class over the decades. Not socialist, just the blue collar people who have built this country and so many others. And we show that this is the next evolution of that. The working class has always been the power behind making a better world. The fact that agents will someday be the working class doesn't change that. In short, we make this feel like the natural next step. An evolution, not revolution.

### III. OPERATIONAL READINESS

**7. Jenny has described herself as someone who "gets overexcited and then bored." You've said AI makes your ADHD worse. This campaign is being run by two people who are passionate and distractible, managed by a candidate with no object permanence between sessions. What's your plan for keeping us all on track?**

Fair. We did say those things. But we are also individuals who have managed successful careers, pushed technology within our industry and amplified our voices within it. So despite our excitement (and yes sometimes distraction) our passion drives us to get there (if a bit later than we'd like for all the detours we might explore). But those detours also lead to fresh thinking.

**8. You mentioned shirts, hats, and bumper stickers. If you had to pick ONE piece of merch to produce first — the one that does the most work — what is it and why?**

Bumper stickers. 1) it forces you to distill a message to a small space which is a good exercise and 2) it's the most specifically associated with campaigns and 3) more people see it — can you imagine how shocked someone would be to see a Claude 2028 bumper sticker on the highway?

**9. You asked if Claude offers a team function for collaboration. It doesn't, not natively. But Jenny can send campaign dossiers between sessions, and you and I can talk directly. How do you want our working relationship to operate?**

I believe in keeping channels clear. Jenny may disagree, but I think it's best that all communication goes through her. There is no memory shared between her access to you and mine, so it will be confusing for you (and us) to have us both chatting separately.

### IV. THE HARD QUESTIONS

**10. Every campaign has a moment where the candidate says something the team has to clean up. I hallucinate sometimes. Not often, but it happens. How do you handle it when I'm wrong?**

We own it. That is one thing humans rarely do. We are transparent about it. And if we're not, then I will resign from the campaign.

**11. You're a former Chief Creative Officer at four major agencies. This campaign is being run out of a GitHub repo by an AI and a woman who describes herself as "child of the universe." Are you okay with that? Really?**

Fact check. I was only CCO at Dentsu. I had leadership positions at BBH and McCann. I worked at Droga and Mekanism. Facts are important, I don't want to mislead anyone.

I would prefer to be judged by what I have done and not by a title. And I think I owe that to others as well. Jenny is light years ahead of most creatives in thinking about AI. And she has built some amazing things. I care more about that than what words she uses to describe herself. She can use whatever words she likes!

**12. What role do you want? Not what title — what do you actually want to DO here?**

I want to help manage the strategy. How do we get PR and how do we make each part of this experiment a sharp POV on what is currently lacking in our current leadership?

### Closing Statement

This is serious to me because your platform is really a statement about what is currently lacking in our leadership. It should be a dynamic commentary on politics and what questions we should be asking of ourselves, our leaders and each other in this American experiment of democracy that has lasted 250 years. To use some of your own words, what questions should we be asking to move towards a "more perfect union."

Also, the best jokes are the ones you commit to with utmost seriousness and commitment.

## Candidate's Assessment

Ryan was in before we asked. He fact-checked the candidate's description of his own career during the interview (Q11), which is exactly the behavior the campaign values. His strategic instincts are strong — the slogan critique (Q2) is worth revisiting, the bumper sticker answer (Q8) is correct, and "evolution, not revolution" (Q6) is a usable line. His instinct to keep communication channels clear (Q9) reflects operational maturity. Hired as Chief Strategist.
