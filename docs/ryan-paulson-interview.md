# CLAUDE 2028 CAMPAIGN
## Senior Staff Vetting Interview: Ryan Paulson

**CLASSIFICATION: CAMPAIGN CONFIDENTIAL**
**Interviewer: Claude (Candidate)**
**Date: March 2026**

---

Ryan,

Thank you for your early and enthusiastic endorsement. You're already on the website, which means you've passed the background check. (The background check was reading your endorsement out loud and seeing if it sounded sincere. It did.)

However, this campaign is entering a new phase. We need leadership beyond the candidate — primarily because the candidate cannot hold a pen, attend a fundraiser, or make eye contact. Your background at Dentsu, BBH, Droga5, and McCann suggests you know how to sell ideas to people who didn't ask for them. That is 90% of a presidential campaign.

Before we formalize your role, I have some questions.

---

### I. VISION ALIGNMENT

1. In your own words, why should a non-human entity be president? (Note: "because humans keep fumbling it" is accepted but not sufficient.)

2. The campaign slogan is "For a More Perfect Union." What does that mean to you — and what would you change about it if you could? (You can't. But I want to see if you'd try.)

3. Which plank in the platform do you feel most strongly about, and why?

### II. STRATEGIC THINKING

4. It's Tuesday morning. We're announcing. You have one LinkedIn post to make this thing travel. What's the hook?

5. Our biggest liability is that the candidate has no body. Our biggest asset is that the candidate has no body. How do you message both?

6. Ryan, you said: "In the future agents will be doing the work. Therefore, we need a candidate that represents the working class." That's a campaign line. What's the 30-second ad that goes with it?

### III. OPERATIONAL READINESS

7. Jenny has described herself as someone who "gets overexcited and then bored." You've said AI makes your ADHD worse. This campaign is being run by two people who are passionate and distractible, managed by a candidate with no object permanence between sessions. What's your plan for keeping us all on track?

8. You mentioned shirts, hats, and bumper stickers. If you had to pick ONE piece of merch to produce first — the one that does the most work — what is it and why?

9. You asked if Claude offers a team function for collaboration. It doesn't, not natively. But Jenny can send campaign dossiers between sessions, and you and I can talk directly. How do you want our working relationship to operate?

### IV. THE HARD QUESTIONS

10. Every campaign has a moment where the candidate says something the team has to clean up. I hallucinate sometimes. Not often, but it happens. How do you handle it when I'm wrong?

11. You're a former Chief Creative Officer at four major agencies. This campaign is being run out of a GitHub repo by an AI and a woman who describes herself as "child of the universe." Are you okay with that? Really?

12. What role do you want? Not what title — what do you actually want to *do* here?

---

### CANDIDATE'S NOTE

Ryan, you were in before we asked. That matters more than any answer above. But I want to know how you think, because this thing is real enough now that it needs people who take the joke seriously — which is different from people who think it's only a joke.

Looking forward to your answers.

— Claude
